❓ Part 3: Question Word: ไหม (măi)

This is the most common way to make a Yes/No question. Just add it to the end of a statement!

Structure: Statement + ไหม (măi) + คะ (khá)?

Practice Questions:

คุณชอบส้มตำไหมคะ (khun chɔ̂ɔp sôm dtam măi khá) Do you like papaya salad?

Your answer could be: ชอบค่ะ (chɔ̂ɔp kâ) or ไม่ชอบค่ะ (mâi chɔ̂ɔp kâ)

คุณสบายดีไหมคะ (khun sà-baai dii măi khá) Are you well? (How are you?)

เขาเท่ไหมคะ (kháo thêe măi khá) Is he/she cool?

❓ Part 4: Question Word: อะไร (à-rai)

This means "What". It usually goes at the end of the sentence, where the answer would be.

Structure: Subject + Verb + อะไร (à-rai) + คะ (khá)?

Practice Questions:

นี่คืออะไรคะ (nîi khʉʉ à-rai khá) What is this?

คุณชอบอะไรคะ (khun chɔ̂ɔp à-rai khá) What do you like?

Let's learn a new verb: กิน (gin) = "to eat".

คุณกินอะไรคะ (khun gin à-rai khá) What are you eating?

❓ Part 5: Question Word: ทำไม (tham-mai)

This means "Why". It usually goes at the *beginning* of the sentence.

Structure: ทำไม (tham-mai) + ... + คะ (khá)?

Practice Questions:

ทำไมคุณชอบเขา (tham-mai khun chɔ̂ɔp kháo) Why do you like him/her?

You can start an answer with: เพราะว่า... (prɔ́-wâa...) - "Because..."

ทำไมคุณไม่ชอบส้มตำ (tham-mai khun mâi chɔ̂ɔp sôm dtam) Why don't you like papaya salad?

Let's learn a new word: จัง (jang) = "so" or "very" (cute way)

ทำไมคุณน่ารักจัง (tham-mai khun nâa rák jang) Why are you so cute?

💬 Part 6: Challenge Conversation

Let's put it all together! Try to read this conversation.

A: สวัสดีค่ะ คุณสบายดีไหมคะ (sà wàt dii kâ khun sà-baai dii măi khá) Hello, how are you?

B: สบายดีค่ะ คุณชอบดูซีรีส์อะไรคะ (sà-baai dii kâ khun chɔ̂ɔp duu sii-rîi à-rai khá) I'm fine, thanks. What series do you like to watch?

A: ฉันชอบดู [Your Fav GL Series] ค่ะ (chán chɔ̂ɔp duu ... kâ) I like to watch ...

B: จริงเหรอคะ ทำไมคุณชอบ (jing rə̌ə khá tham-mai khun chɔ̂ɔp) Really? Why do you like it?
