Telling Time in Thai

Learning the "Helper Words" (Tense Participles)

💖 Part 1: Quick Review

Let's remember our question words. We need them today!

"What?" is อะไร (à-rai)

"Why?" is ทำไม (tham-mai)

💡 Part 2: The Big Idea!

This is the most important rule: Thai verbs never change!

In English, you say "eat", "ate", "eating". In Thai, the verb กิน (gin) is always กิน (gin).

To show time, we add small "helper words" (participles) around the verb. Let's learn them!

🚀 Part 3: The Future - จะ (jà) "will"

Place จะ (jà) before the verb to show the future.

จะทำอะไร (jà tham à-rai) What will (you) do?

ฉันจะกิน (chán jà gin) I will eat.

🏃‍♀️ Part 4: The Present - ...อยู่ (yùu) "...ing"

Place อยู่ (yùu) after the verb to show it's happening right now.

ทำอะไรอยู่ (tham à-rai yùu) What are (you) doing?

ฉันกินอยู่ (chán gin yùu) I am eating (right now).

✅ Part 5: The Past - ...แล้ว (láew) "already"

Place แล้ว (láew) at the end of a sentence to show it's completed.

กินข้าวแล้ว (gin khâao láew) (I) have eaten (already).

เขาไปแล้ว (kháo bpai láew) He/She went (already).

⏪ Part 6: The Recent Past - ...มา (maa)

Place มา (maa) after the verb to talk about a recent action. (It literally means "come".)

ทำอะไรมา (tham à-rai maa) What have you been doing? / What did you just do?

ไปไหนมา (bpai năi maa) Where have you been? (A very common greeting!)

❔ Part 7: "Yet?" & "Not Yet" - ...ยัง (yang)

This word is used for both the question and the negative answer!

A: The Question "...yet?"

Place ยัง (yang) at the end of a question.

กินข้าวยัง (gin khâao yang) Have (you) eaten yet? (Casual)

B: The Answer "Not yet..."

Start the answer with ยัง (yang), then add ไม่ (mâi) before the verb.

ยังไม่กิน (yang mâi gin) (I) haven't eaten yet.

ยังไม่ทำ (yang mâi tham) (I) haven't done it yet.

🚀 Part 8: Extension Activities

What's the Time Word? Fill in the blank.

1. "I ___ go." (I *will* go.)

ฉัน ___ ไป (chán ___ bpai)

2. "I'm working (right now)."

ฉันทำงาน ___ (chán tham-ngaan ___)

3. "I haven't eaten (yet)."

___ ไม่กิน (___ mâi gin)

4. "She left (already)."

เขาไป ___ (kháo bpai ___)

ภารกิจ (phaa-rá-gìt) (Mission)

Try to make one sentence for each time word! For example:
ฉันจะดูซีรีส์ (chán jà duu sii-rîi) - "I will watch a series."
